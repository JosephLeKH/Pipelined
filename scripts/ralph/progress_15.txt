## US-209 — Fix calendar date range off-by-one at month boundaries
**Status:** COMPLETE
**Commit:** feat: US-209 - Fix calendar date range off-by-one at month boundaries

**Summary:**
Replaced fragile modular arithmetic `today.month % 12 + 1` / `today.month // 12` with `calendar.monthrange(today.year, today.month)[1]` in `backend/cal/service.py`. The old code computed the last day of the month by getting the first day of next month then subtracting one day — brittle at December/year boundaries. The new approach directly returns the last day of the current month using the stdlib `calendar` module.

**Changes:**
- Added `import calendar` to `backend/cal/service.py`
- Replaced 4-line `today.replace(day=1, month=..., year=...) - timedelta(days=1)` with 2-line `calendar.monthrange()` approach

**Learnings:**
- Pre-existing test failures exist in `test_calendar.py` (8 failures) and `test_applications.py` (1 failure) — these predate this story and are unrelated to the date range fix.
- `calendar.monthrange(year, month)` returns `(weekday_of_first_day, number_of_days)` — index `[1]` gives the last day.
